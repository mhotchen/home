Dartmoor (2020-08-01)

Hello again.

I've been meaning to try combining two of my hobbies: motorcycling and camping, and I'd been itching to get away for a little while. It turns out my 20l bag can hold quite a lot! I managed to squeeze an overnight camp in to it, including two sleeping mats!

I set off to Dartmoor on Saturday afternoon, taking the simplest way possible which I committed to memory. It definitely wasn't the most exciting route, mostly high speed A-roads through the country-side through farmland with the occasional stunning view. I was impressed by Stonehenge though and a couple of the bigger hills offered some nice views and a few twists.

I arrived in Okehampton around 7pm, grabbed a proper fish and chips which is frustratingly difficult to find in London, and took a breather. My phone didn't have 4g in Okehampton which caught me by surprise and I didn't bring any paper maps. I had enough tiles cached in Google Maps to figure out my route then headed up the road to Dartmoor national park. It was a very pleasant little road, just slow and lovely, head nods and waves to passerbys. I got to the carpark with plenty of sunlight. I chained my helmet to my bike and took off up a flat, dull looking hill out of sight to set up camp.

There was to be some small showers at 7am, but they arrived much earlier and lasted longer than I expected. Also, because I always like to camp at the top of hills for some reason (maybe because of the time I nearly got washed away in my tent in a Scottish winter) it tends to be windy, and there was no exception that night. Still, my shelter held up and I felt comfortable. I woke up and the shower was gone so I could camly pack up again. The local sheep weren't too happy to see me pop up in the middle of them, and one got a bit territorial when I was trying to take a leek.

I got lost twice, both times trying to figure out the M5 system. I ended up on a lovely country road the second time round so no regrets.

I think because I was tired I was getting angry on the way home. About halfway through the return journey I stopped for a break, recognised my anger, and relaxed again.

It was a pretty tough ride just for a few hours kip on a mountain before making the return trip again.

Things I learned

  • My core took a battering from spending so long at 60+mph
  • The roads in the UK are not always very clearly signed
  • I need to think more about my navigation setup
  • Again, very simple to achieve
